A prototype to help the Digital Jobcentres team research people's experiences in checking in for scheduled appointments.

I'm interested in:

  • people's ability to enter their details due to the size and location of the device in the jobcentre
  • how people write their names compared to those held in DWP's records - eg name ordering, parts of name possibly being omitted or written incorrectly
  • how people enter special characters
  • how little we need to gather in order to identify someone, when there could be hundreds of appointments
  • how reliably we can direct people to waiting areas, if the number and location of these are different in every jobcentre
  • how we design for people with accessibility needs
  • how we design for people with low literacy, or who speak and read a different language
  • how we can test sending staff a message once someone completes the form

Phil Swan suggests we observe people trying to use the GP check-in system - what issues do people have with their approach? Arrange with local practices.

Initial pages

  • If they have an appointment today
  • Name
  • Date of birth
  • Details do not match an appointment record - check answers

Possible outcome pages

  • Has an appointment, is on time
  • Has an appointment, is late
  • Cannot find appointment
  • Does not have an appointment
